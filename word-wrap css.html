The word-wrap property in CSS commonly known as overflow-wrap is used to handle how words are broken and wrapped onto the next line when they are too long to fit within their container. This is particularly useful when dealing with long words or strings of text that might otherwise overflow their container.

1.word-wrap (Deprecated) Values normal: Words will only break at normal line break points (default behavior). break-word: Words that are too long to fit will break at arbitrary points to prevent overflow.


2.overflow-wrap (Current Standard) Values normal: Breaks only at allowed points (same as word-wrap: normal). nbreak-word: Breaks long words to prevent overflow (same as word-wrap: break-word).

No Word Wrap (normal):

ThisIsAReallyLongWordThatWillNotBreakEvenIfItOverflowsItsContainerWithoutWordWrap.

Word Wrap (break-word):
